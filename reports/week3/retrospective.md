# Sprint Retrospective

## What went well
- **Successful Prototype Demonstration**: The team successfully demonstrated functioning prototypes for both "Color Match" and "Spell Caster" games, including a working core voice recognition system that accurately transcribed speech during the meeting.
- **Positive Customer Feedback on Core Loop**: The customer liked the core ideas of both games, approved the gameplay loops, and validated the chosen pixelated visual style for Spell Caster.
- **Effective Problem Solving During Review**: The team successfully proposed on-the-spot solutions, such as the 10-second "dipping brush" timer for auto-painting, which the customer immediately approved.

## What did not go well
- **Resource Bottleneck**: Relying on a single developer for prototyping created a significant bottleneck, negatively impacting the team's velocity.
- **Inefficient Voice Mechanics**: The initial design of using a constantly listening microphone and checking pronunciation against a massive list of words simultaneously proved inefficient and tedious for the user.
- **Misaligned Loot System Design**: The proposed "chest" loot system for Spell Caster did not meet the customer's expectations and was rejected in favor of direct loot drops from defeated monsters.

## Action points
- **Distribute Prototyping Load**: All team members will start prototyping game concepts independently in Google AI Studio to eliminate the single-developer bottleneck and increase overall team velocity.
- **Implement "Select-then-Speak" Mechanic**: Transition from a constantly listening microphone to a "select-then-speak" mechanic across all games to improve system performance and usability.
